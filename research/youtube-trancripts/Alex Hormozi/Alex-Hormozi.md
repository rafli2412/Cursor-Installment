## Best Use Cases for B2B SaaS

### 1. Founder-led YouTube strategy
Alex Hormozi’s content is most useful for B2B SaaS when the founder, CEO, or subject-matter expert becomes the face of the channel. The main takeaway is to build trust by repeatedly teaching the market, not by making every video a direct product pitch.

### 2. Content engine design
The selected videos can help SaaS teams think about content as a system: long-form videos, short-form clips, repeated themes, strong hooks, and consistent distribution. This fits well for SaaS companies that need ongoing demand generation rather than one-off campaign content.

### 3. Authority and category education
For B2B SaaS, the strongest application is educational content that helps buyers understand a problem, compare approaches, and trust the company’s perspective before booking a demo.

---

## Practical B2B SaaS Adaptation

A SaaS team could adapt these ideas into a YouTube strategy like this:

1. Pick 3–5 recurring content pillars around the buyer’s pain points.
2. Publish founder-led or expert-led long-form videos that teach the market.
3. Repurpose each video into Shorts, LinkedIn clips, newsletter sections, and sales enablement assets.
4. Use titles and hooks focused on outcomes, mistakes, frameworks, and buyer objections.
5. Connect content to soft conversion points such as demos, templates, webinars, free tools, or email courses.

Use Alex Hormozi’s channel as a reference for **content velocity, authority-building, and founder-led audience growth**, not as a direct B2B SaaS YouTube playbook.
